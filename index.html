Shuang Quan, Ph.D.

I am an Assistant Professor of Education at Juniata College. I earned my Ph.D. in Innovation in Curriculum and Instruction from Fordham University's Graduate School of Education, with a professional background in early childhood education and literacy education. My research focuses on designing, implementing, and evaluating emerging technologies, particularly Artificial Intelligence (AI) and Large Language Models, to support young children's literacy development.

I founded the AI in Education Lab (AEL), where I lead interdisciplinary studies exploring how conversational AI tools can support language, cognitive, and social-emotional development for young children. I am also committed and actively engaged in curriculum development to prepare future educators to become AI-literate and AI-competent in contemporary classrooms.

AI in Education Lab (AEL)

My work centers on the intersection of artificial intelligence and education, with a particular focus on early childhood development. Through innovative research and practical applications, I explore how emerging technologies can enhance learning experiences while preparing educators for the digital future.
