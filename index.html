Shuang Quan, Ph.D.

Assistant Professor of Education

Director of AI in Education Lab

I am an Assistant Professor of Education at Juniata College. I earned my Ph.D. in Innovation in Curriculum and Instruction from Fordham University's Graduate School of Education, with a professional background in Early Childhood Education and Literacy Education. My research focuses on designing, implementing, and evaluating emerging technologies, particularly Artificial Intelligence (AI) and Large Language Models (LLMs), to support young children's literacy development, especially those from diverse linguistic and socioeconomic backgrounds.

I founded the AI in Education Lab (AEL), where I lead interdisciplinary studies exploring how conversational AI tools can support language, cognitive, and social-emotional development for young children. I am also committed to and actively engaged in developing curriculum for teacher preparation programs to equip future educators to become AI-literate and AI-competent in contemporary classrooms.

AI in Education Lab (AEL)

The AI in Education Lab (AEL), launched in December 2024, brings together talented student researchers from Education and Computer Science to collaboratively design, develop, and implement AI-powered tools that support the language and cognitive development of young children from diverse linguistic and socioeconomic backgrounds.

Early Literacy Development

Developing innovative approaches to support language and literacy development in early childhood education settings.

Educator Preparation

Preparing future educators to integrate AI technologies effectively and ethically in their teaching practice.

AEL team members facilitated the Language & AI Panel at the EAP 30th Anniversary Celebration, held at the Statton Learning Center at Juniata, April 2025.
